MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Master Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of March 1, 2025 (the "Effective Date") by and between NovaTech Solutions Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Provider"), and Meridian Global Partners LLC, a New York limited liability company ("Client").

SECTION 1: DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Services" means the consulting, development, and technical support services described in one or more Statements of Work ("SOWs") executed under this Agreement.

1.2 "Confidential Information" means any non-public technical, business, financial, or other information disclosed by either party, whether in written, oral, electronic, or visual form.

1.3 "Deliverables" means the tangible work products, reports, software, documentation, and other materials produced by Provider in the course of performing the Services.

1.4 "Intellectual Property Rights" means all patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, and other intellectual property rights recognized in any jurisdiction.

SECTION 2: SCOPE OF SERVICES

2.1 Provider shall perform the Services described in each SOW in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with generally accepted industry standards.

2.2 Each SOW shall specify: (a) a description of the Services; (b) the timeline and milestones; (c) fees and payment schedule; (d) any Client-provided resources; and (e) acceptance criteria.

2.3 Provider shall assign qualified personnel to perform the Services. Provider may, with Client's prior written consent, engage subcontractors, provided that Provider remains responsible for the work performed by such subcontractors.

SECTION 3: COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT

3.1 Client shall pay Provider the fees set forth in each SOW. Unless otherwise stated, all fees are in United States Dollars.

3.2 Provider shall submit invoices monthly in arrears. Payment is due within thirty (30) days of receipt of a proper invoice.

3.3 Late payments shall accrue interest at the rate of 1.5% per month, or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is less.

3.4 All fees are exclusive of applicable taxes. Client shall be responsible for all sales, use, and similar taxes, excluding taxes based on Provider's income.

SECTION 4: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

4.1 All pre-existing intellectual property remains the property of the party that owned it prior to the Effective Date.

4.2 Subject to full payment of all fees, Provider hereby assigns to Client all right, title, and interest in and to the Deliverables, including all Intellectual Property Rights therein.

4.3 Provider retains the right to use general knowledge, skills, experience, and methodologies acquired during the performance of Services, provided that such use does not disclose Client's Confidential Information.

SECTION 5: CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 Each party agrees to hold the other party's Confidential Information in strict confidence and to use it solely for the purposes of this Agreement.

5.2 The obligations under this Section shall survive for a period of five (5) years following the termination or expiration of this Agreement.

5.3 Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party; (b) was rightfully known by the receiving party prior to disclosure; (c) is independently developed without use of Confidential Information; or (d) is required to be disclosed by law or regulation.

SECTION 6: REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

6.1 Provider represents and warrants that: (a) the Services will be performed in a professional manner; (b) the Deliverables will conform to the specifications set forth in the applicable SOW; and (c) the Deliverables will not infringe upon the intellectual property rights of any third party.

6.2 Client represents and warrants that it has the authority to enter into this Agreement and that any materials provided to Provider for use in the Services do not infringe upon the intellectual property rights of any third party.

6.3 EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH IN THIS AGREEMENT, NEITHER PARTY MAKES ANY WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WITHOUT LIMITATION ANY WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

SECTION 7: INDEMNIFICATION

7.1 Provider shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client against any third-party claims arising from: (a) Provider's breach of this Agreement; (b) Provider's negligence or willful misconduct; or (c) any claim that the Deliverables infringe a third party's Intellectual Property Rights.

7.2 Client shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Provider against any third-party claims arising from: (a) Client's breach of this Agreement; (b) Client's use of the Deliverables in a manner not contemplated by the applicable SOW; or (c) any materials provided by Client that infringe a third party's rights.

7.3 The indemnified party shall: (a) provide prompt written notice of any claim; (b) grant the indemnifying party sole control of the defense and settlement; and (c) provide reasonable cooperation at the indemnifying party's expense.

SECTION 8: LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

8.1 IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO LOSS OF PROFITS, REVENUE, DATA, OR BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY, REGARDLESS OF THE CAUSE OF ACTION OR THEORY OF LIABILITY.

8.2 The exclusions in Section 8.1 shall not apply to: (a) a party's indemnification obligations under Section 7; (b) a party's breach of its confidentiality obligations under Section 5; or (c) either party's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

8.3 EXCEPT FOR THE EXCLUSIONS IN SECTION 8.2, THE TOTAL AGGREGATE LIABILITY OF EITHER PARTY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED TWO MILLION FIVE HUNDRED THOUSAND DOLLARS ($2,500,000).

8.4 The limitations in this Section 8 shall apply regardless of whether the damages are foreseeable and regardless of whether the party has been advised of the possibility of such damages.

SECTION 9: TERM AND TERMINATION

9.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of three (3) years, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Section 9.

9.2 Either party may terminate this Agreement: (a) for convenience upon sixty (60) days' prior written notice; or (b) for cause if the other party commits a material breach and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days of receiving written notice thereof.

9.3 Upon termination: (a) Client shall pay all fees for Services performed through the termination date; (b) Provider shall deliver all completed and in-progress Deliverables; and (c) each party shall return or destroy all Confidential Information of the other party.

9.4 Sections 4, 5, 7, 8, and 10 shall survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.

SECTION 10: GENERAL PROVISIONS

10.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of New York, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

10.2 Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall be resolved by binding arbitration in New York City, administered by the American Arbitration Association under its Commercial Arbitration Rules.

10.3 This Agreement, together with all SOWs, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior oral or written agreements relating to the subject matter hereof.

10.4 Neither party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other party, except that either party may assign this Agreement to a successor in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets.

10.5 No modification of this Agreement shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by both parties.

10.6 If any provision of this Agreement is found to be unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

10.7 All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses specified in the applicable SOW.
